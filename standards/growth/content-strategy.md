# Content Strategy Standards

> Reference document for the content-engine skill. Defines quality bars, format guidelines, SEO requirements, and performance benchmarks for all content marketing outputs.

## Content Formats and When to Use Each

| Format | Length | When to Use | Goal |
|--------|--------|-------------|------|
| **Blog Post** | 800-2000 words | Educate on a topic, target a keyword, establish expertise | Organic traffic, brand authority |
| **Tutorial** | 1500-3000 words | Show how to accomplish a task step-by-step | Product adoption, long-tail SEO |
| **Case Study** | 1000-1500 words | Prove value with a real customer story | Bottom-of-funnel conversion |
| **Comparison** | 1200-2000 words | Compare against competitors or alternatives | Capture competitor search traffic |
| **Newsletter** | 500-800 words | Curate and summarize recent content/updates | Retention, repeat engagement |
| **Video Script** | 3-10 min runtime | Visual demonstration, thought leadership | Engagement, brand awareness |

### Format Selection Rules

- **Top of funnel** (awareness): Blog posts, tutorials, video scripts
- **Middle of funnel** (consideration): Comparisons, case studies, newsletters
- **Bottom of funnel** (decision): Case studies, comparisons with CTAs, product tutorials
- **Retention**: Newsletters, advanced tutorials, changelog digests

## Writing Quality Standards

### Readability

- Target grade level: **8-10** (Flesch-Kincaid). Write clearly enough for a broad professional audience.
- Sentence length: average **15-20 words**. Break long sentences into two.
- Paragraph length: **2-4 sentences** maximum. One idea per paragraph.
- Use **active voice** by default. Passive voice is acceptable only when the actor is irrelevant.

### Structure and Scannability

- Every piece must have a clear **H1 title** (one per page)
- Use **H2 and H3 subheadings** every 200-300 words
- Use **bullet points and numbered lists** for sequences, features, and comparisons
- Include a **TL;DR or key takeaway** in the first 100 words
- Bold **key terms** on first use in each section
- Use tables for structured data comparisons (never write comparison data as prose)

### Tone and Voice

- Value before promotion: **80% useful content, 20% product mention** (maximum)
- Write in second person ("you") to address the reader directly
- Avoid jargon unless the audience expects it (developer content can use technical terms)
- No filler phrases: "In today's world...", "It goes without saying...", "At the end of the day..."
- Be specific: use numbers, timeframes, and concrete examples instead of vague claims

### Quality Gates

- [ ] Passes readability check (grade 8-10)
- [ ] Active voice throughout (>90% of sentences)
- [ ] Scannable with headers every 200-300 words
- [ ] No filler phrases
- [ ] Value ratio: 80% educational / 20% promotional (max)
- [ ] Every claim backed by data, example, or logic

## SEO Content Checklist

### On-Page SEO Requirements

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Title tag** | 50-60 characters, includes target keyword, front-loaded |
| **Meta description** | 150-160 characters, includes target keyword, has a CTA or value prop |
| **H1** | Matches or closely reflects the title tag, contains target keyword |
| **URL slug** | Short, lowercase, hyphen-separated, includes target keyword (e.g., `/blog/content-strategy-guide`) |
| **H2/H3 headings** | At least one subheading includes a secondary keyword naturally |
| **Alt text** | Every image has descriptive alt text (not keyword-stuffed) |
| **Internal links** | Minimum 2-3 internal links to related content within the same topic cluster |
| **External links** | 1-2 authoritative external references where relevant |
| **Target keyword density** | 1-2% of total word count (natural usage, not forced) |

### Technical SEO

- Canonical URL set on every page
- Open Graph tags for social sharing (og:title, og:description, og:image)
- Schema markup where applicable (Article, HowTo, FAQ)
- Page load under 3 seconds (optimize images, lazy load below-fold content)

### Keyword Targeting Rules

- **One primary keyword per piece** (avoid cannibalization across posts)
- **2-3 secondary keywords** per piece (related terms, long-tail variations)
- Primary keyword appears in: title, H1, first 100 words, at least one H2, meta description
- Secondary keywords appear naturally in body copy and subheadings
- Check seo-topic-map output to confirm keyword-to-content assignments

## Content Multiplication Rules

### Minimum 5 Derivatives Per Pillar Piece

Every pillar content piece (blog post, tutorial, case study) must generate at least 5 derivative assets:

1. **3 Social Posts**: One per active social platform (adapt tone per platform)
2. **1 Newsletter Entry**: Summary paragraph + link for the next newsletter issue
3. **1 Email Sequence Asset**: Content excerpt or lesson for an onboarding/nurture drip
4. **1 Community Post** (optional but recommended): Value-first version for Reddit, Hacker News, or relevant forums
5. **1 Video Script Outline** (optional): Talking points for a 3-5 minute video version

### Derivative Quality Rules

- Each derivative must **stand alone** -- readers should get value without clicking through
- Adapt **tone and format** per platform (professional on LinkedIn, concise on Twitter/X, visual on Instagram)
- Spread derivatives across the week: do not publish all derivatives on the same day as the pillar piece
- Track derivative creation in the editorial calendar (status column)

### Social Post Specifications

| Platform | Length | Format | Tone |
|----------|--------|--------|------|
| **Twitter/X** | 240 chars or thread (3-5 tweets) | Single post or numbered thread | Concise, punchy, hook-first |
| **LinkedIn** | 500-1300 chars | Paragraph format, line breaks for readability | Professional, insights-driven |
| **Instagram** | 150-300 chars caption + visual | Carousel (5-10 slides) or single image card | Visual-first, accessible |

## Performance Benchmarks by Content Type

### Traffic Benchmarks (Monthly, Per Piece, After 3 Months)

| Content Type | Minimum | Good | Excellent |
|-------------|---------|------|-----------|
| Blog Post | 200 views | 1,000 views | 5,000+ views |
| Tutorial | 300 views | 2,000 views | 10,000+ views |
| Case Study | 100 views | 500 views | 2,000+ views |
| Comparison | 500 views | 3,000 views | 15,000+ views |

### Engagement Benchmarks

| Metric | Minimum | Good | Excellent |
|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| Avg. time on page | 1:30 min | 3:00 min | 5:00+ min |
| Scroll depth | 40% | 60% | 80%+ |
| Social shares | 5 | 25 | 100+ |
| Comments | 1 | 5 | 20+ |

### Conversion Benchmarks

| Metric | Minimum | Good | Excellent |
|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| CTA click-through | 1% | 3% | 7%+ |
| Email signup from content | 0.5% | 2% | 5%+ |
| Demo request attribution | 0.1% | 0.5% | 2%+ |

### SEO Benchmarks (6 Months After Publish)

| Metric | Minimum | Good | Excellent |
|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| Target keyword ranking | Page 3+ | Page 1 (pos 4-10) | Page 1 (pos 1-3) |
| Organic traffic share | 20% of piece traffic | 50% | 80%+ |
| Backlinks earned | 0 | 3-5 | 10+ |

### Review and Refresh Cadence

- **Weekly**: Review performance of content published in the last 7 days
- **Monthly**: Identify top 3 and bottom 3 performers; adjust editorial calendar priorities
- **Quarterly**: Full content audit -- refresh underperformers (update stats, improve SEO, add new sections), prune or consolidate content that is not ranking and not driving traffic
- **Content refresh trigger**: Any piece that drops below "Minimum" benchmarks after 6 months should be refreshed or consolidated
